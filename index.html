History of Life

Life on earth is incredibly diverse, with many different kinds of animals inhabiting the sea, land, and waterways of our planet. The diversity of life that we currently have on earth is the result of hundreds of millions of years of evolution, with whole ecosystems rising and falling through geological upheaval, mass extinction events, and the constant push of natural selection.

While the vast majority of creatures that have walked, swum, crawled, or squirmed across the face of this planet have been consigned to prehistory, their fossilised remains give us a window into what life on our planet looked like in the past. The study of these fossils and the animals they represent is known as paleontology.

Given the vast period of time that complex life has existed on this planet, when talking about geological and biological history, the ~600 million years since complex life started evolving is split into 3 era: the Paleozoic, the Mesozoic, and the Cenozoic.

Each of these periods is characterised by different forms of life, and boundaries between them are marked by massive ecological upheaval.
